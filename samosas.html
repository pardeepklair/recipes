Indian Recipes

A deep fried pastry with a spiced filling usually made with potatoes, spices and herbs. S Sometimes these are also made with minced meat (keema). Samosa is one of the most commonly eaten snack across India.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, crushed ginger, & red chilli powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 2 tablespoon ghee
  • 2 cup refined oil
  • 5 boiled potatoes

    2. Put a pan on medium flame and add 2 tsp oil in it. Once the oil is hot enough, add cumin seeds and allow them to crackle.

  2. Add spices and boiled potatoes and cook for a while

    3. Add whole coriander seeds, ginger and green chilligaram masala powder, red chilli powder, salt to taste. Mix well and saute for 2 minute.

  3. Prepare the dough for the Samosa

    4. Take a mixing bowl and combine all-purpose flour with salt. Mix and then add ghee and start kneading by adding a little water at a time. Ensure that you add water gradually and make a firm dough. A soft dough will not make your samosas crispy.

  4. Roll the dough in small puris and cut into half

    5. Once done, roll out few small-sized balls from the dough. Flatten them further with the help of your palms and then with a rolling pin

  5. Fill the semi-circle with potato filling and deep fry

    6. Take the filling with the help of a spoon and stuff it in the cone. Seal the ends properly by pressing the edges lightly with your fingers. Then, heat oil in a pan and deep fry the samosas on low heat until golden brown.
